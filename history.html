BMW M History

BMW Motorsport was founded in 1972 with only 35 employees but as the company grew the number got to 400 in 1988. It was specifically for managing BMWs racing program, it then evolved from the track to produce high end performance road cars.

    BMW 3.0 CSL

    The BMW 3.0 CSL (Coupe Sport Lightweight) was a homologation special of the 1973 touring car championship. It featured a 3.1L inline-6 producing 206hp, this symbolizes how far BMW has come with its inline-6 engines and how much they have been a success as the BMW 3.0 CSL went on to win 6 titles in the touring car championship and several class wins at Le Mans.

    BMW M1

    The M1 is a very special car, the first and only mid-engined car from BMW. It has a 3.5L inline-6 with a power output of 277hp. The M1 is widely known for its role of starting the BMW M division, there are only 453 units of this magnificent beauty worldwide produced from 1978 to 1981. Which makes in one of the rarest BMWs, it deserves that title.
